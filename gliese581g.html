ABOUT

On September 29, 2010, astronomers using the Keck Observatory discovered the planet Gliese 581g in nearly circular orbits based on an analysis of a combination of datasets from the HARPS and HIRES instruments.

Gliese 581g is an exoplanet in the Gliese 581 star system. Gliese 581 is an M-type red dwarf star. Such stars are usually known for their low temperature and brightness. Gliese 581g has attracted attention because it is located in the star's habitable zone.

While the radius of Gliese 581g has not been precisely determined, its mass is thought to be about 3 to 4 times the mass of Earth. This means that the planet's radius could also be larger than Earth's radius. But it is difficult to give an exact figure because such calculations are usually based on indirect data. General estimates suggest that its radius could be between 1.1 and 1.5 times that of Earth's.
